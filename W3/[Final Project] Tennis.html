Brief history

In 1873, Londoner Major Walter Wingfield invented a game called he called Sphairistikè (Greek for "playing ball"). Played on an hourglass-shaped court, Wingfield's game created a sensation in Europe, the United States, and even China, and is the source from which tennis as we know it today eventually evolved.


About tennis

Tennis is a racket sport that can be played individually against a single opponent (singles) or between two teams of two players each (doubles).


Fun facts about tennis

🎾 The modern game of tennis originated in Birmingham, England, in the late 19th century as “lawn tennis”.

🎾 There are four Grand Slam tournaments: The Australian Open, The French Open, Wimbledon and the US Open.

🎾 Venus and Serena Williams became the first set of sisters to ever win Olympic gold medals in tennis.
